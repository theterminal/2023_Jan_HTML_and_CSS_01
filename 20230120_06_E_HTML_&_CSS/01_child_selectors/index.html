Adjacent Sibling Combinator

Example 3

'.example p + p' selects all paragraphs directly after a paragraph element on the same level.

            
    .container-adjacent p + p{
        background-color: orange;
        padding: 5px;
    }

I am paragraph #1 and I am NOT selected by 'p + p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT afrer 'p' elemnt.

I am paragraph #2 and I am selected by 'p + p' selector in the CSS, because I am adjacent sibling 'p' element after 'p' element.

I am paragraph #3 and I am also selected by 'p + p' selector in the CSS, because I am adjacent sibling 'p' element after 'p' element.

I am a DIV element.

I am paragraph #4 and I am NOT selected by 'p + p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT directly after 'p' element.

I am paragraph #5 and I am also selected by 'p + p' selector in the CSS, because I am adjacent sibling 'p' element after 'p' element.

Example 4

'.example div + p' selects the first 'p' directly after a 'div' element on the same level.

            
    .container-adjacent-2 div + p{
        background-color: red;
        padding: 5px;
    }

I am 'p' #1 and I am NOT selected by 'div + p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT after 'div' elemnt.

I am 'p' #2 and I am NOT selected by 'div + p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT after 'div' element.

I am 'p' #3 and I am NOT selected by 'div + p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT after 'div' element.

I am a DIV element.

I am 'p' #4 and I am selected by 'div + p' selector in the CSS, because I am after 'p' element.

I am 'p' #5 and I am NOT selected by 'div + p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT after 'div' element.

General Sibling Combinator

Example 5

'.example p ~ p' selects all 'p' after a 'p' element on the same level.

            
    .container-general p ~ p{
        background-color: green;
        color: white;
        padding: 5px;
    }

I am paragraph #1 and I am NOT selected by 'p ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT afrer 'p' elemnt.

I am paragraph #2 and I am selected by 'p ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am 'p' element after 'p' element.

I am paragraph #3 and I am also selected by 'p ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am 'p' element after 'p' element.

I am a DIV element.

I am paragraph #4 and I am selected by 'p ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am 'p' element after 'p' element. It doesn't matter that I am not directly after the previous 'p' element.

I am paragraph #5 and I am selected by 'p ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am 'p' element after 'p' element.

Example 6

'.example div ~ p' selects all paragraphs directly after a 'div' element on the same level.

            
    .container-general-2 div ~ p{
        background-color: rgb(105, 114, 87);
        padding: 5px;
        color: #fff;
    }

I am 'p' #1 and I am NOT selected by 'div ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT afrer 'div' elemnt.

I am 'p' #2 and I am NOT selected by 'div ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT after 'div' element.

I am 'p' #3 and I am NOT selected by 'div ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am NOT after 'div' element.

I am a DIV element.

I am 'p' #4 and I am selected by 'div ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am after 'p' element.

I am 'p' #5 and I am selected by 'div ~ p' selector in the CSS, because I am after 'div' element.
